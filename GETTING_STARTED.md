# Getting Started

This guide covers the early Arcanum loop: build your forge, craft your first CAD, unlock new sigils, and grow your mana pool so your spells stop fizzling out.

If you only read one page before jumping in, read this one.

## What You Are Working Toward

Your first stretch of progression looks like this:

1. craft the `Arcanum Forge`
2. craft a `Copper CAD`
3. build a first simple spell
4. farm codices from enemies and chests
5. unlock more nodes
6. upgrade into better CAD tiers as your spells grow

## Step 1: Craft the Arcanum Forge

The `Arcanum Forge` is your first real milestone. It is where your spellcasting progression starts to become its own system.

You use it to:

- craft spells
- manage the slots of your CAD

| Item | Materials |
| --- | --- |
| Arcanum Forge | 20 Rock, 5 Gold Bars, 20 Crystal, 10 Bone Fragments |

Craft it at a normal crafting workbench, then place it somewhere convenient. You will come back to it often.

## Step 2: Craft Your First CAD

Start with the `Copper CAD`.

This is your first proper casting device. It gives you enough room for simple spell layouts and introduces the core CAD progression: more slots, higher complexity, and better efficiency.

| CAD | Materials | Slots | Complexity | Efficiency | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Copper CAD | 6 Copper Bars, 2 Heavy Leather, 20 Crystal | 3 | 32 | 100% | Starting tier |
| Bronze CAD | Copper CAD, 8 Bronze Bars, 2 Heavy Leather, 25 Crystal | 4 | 44 | 108% | First upgrade |
| Iron CAD | Bronze CAD, 10 Iron Bars, 30 Crystal, 6 Void Essence | 6 | 58 | 118% | Good midgame jump |
| Gold CAD | Iron CAD, 10 Gold Bars, 35 Crystal, 8 Void Essence | 7 | 74 | 130% | Strong general-purpose tier |
| Thorium CAD | Gold CAD, 12 Thorium Bars, 40 Crystal, 16 Void Essence | 9 | 92 | 145% | Requires Forge Tier 2 |
| Cobalt CAD | Thorium CAD, 14 Cobalt Bars, 45 Crystal, 12 Void Essence | 10 | 112 | 162% | Requires Forge Tier 2 |
| Adamantite CAD | Cobalt CAD, 16 Adamantite Bars, 50 Crystal, 20 Void Essence | 12 | 136 | 182% | Requires Forge Tier 3 |

Higher-tier CADs do three important things:

- give you more slots to work with
- allow more complex spell graphs
- improve cast efficiency

If a spell feels cramped, too expensive, or too advanced for your current device, you are probably hitting a CAD limit rather than doing anything wrong.

## Step 3: Learn Your Starter Sigils

When you begin, you already know a small starter set of default pieces. That starter kit is enough to make your first working spell and learn how the Spell Crafter grid behaves.

Your default pool includes:

- `Invoke Self`
- `Self`
- `Number Sigil`
- `Combine`
- `Subtract`
- `Amplify`
- `Unleash Ruin`
- `Mend Essence`

For the full list of unlocks and where they come from, see [PIECES.md](PIECES.md).

## Step 4: Hunt for Codices

New spell pieces are unlocked through `Arcanum Codices`.

- Kill enemies and loot chests to find codices.
- Right-click a codex to unlock one new node from that codex pool.
- Logic codices unlock math, utility, and control pieces.
- Effect codices unlock new spell effects.
- Trigger codices unlock targeting, sensing, and cast setup pieces.

Think of codices as the main way your spellbook expands. Your early loop is:

1. craft better gear
2. hunt codices
3. unlock more sigils
4. build stronger spells
5. repeat with a better CAD

## Step 5: Grow Your Mana by Unlocking Nodes

Mana does not scale from gear alone. Your knowledge matters too.

Unlocking more nodes increases your max mana at specific milestones:

| Unlocked Nodes | Max Mana |
| --- | --- |
| 0 | 100 |
| 6 | 125 |
| 12 | 150 |
| 20 | 180 |
| 28 | 220 |
| 36 | 270 |
| 44 | 330 |
| 52 | 400 |
| 60 | 500 |

This means node progression is not just about variety. It directly increases how much spellcasting power you can hold.

If you want to cast larger spells reliably, keep collecting codices. More unlocks means more options and a larger mana reserve.

## First Goals

If you are just starting out, this is a good first-session checklist:

1. Craft the Arcanum Forge.
2. Craft a Copper CAD.
3. Build one simple self-heal spell to learn the editor.
4. Start farming codices from enemies and chests.
5. Upgrade into Bronze or Iron as soon as your spell layouts start feeling cramped.
